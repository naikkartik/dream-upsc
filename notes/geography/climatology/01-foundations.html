Atmospheric Foundations

The basic building blocks — what the atmosphere is made of, how it's layered, how heat enters and leaves Earth, and how temperature changes with altitude. Master these first; everything else builds on them.

01
Earth's Atmosphere — Composition & Layers
PDF · Ch 20 PYQ 2018, 2021, 2024
+

Definition

The atmosphere is the gaseous envelope (~10,000 km thick) around Earth, held by gravity. Dry air = 78% N₂, 21% O₂, 0.93% Argon, 0.04% CO₂ + trace gases (CH₄, N₂O, O₃) + variable water vapour (0–4%).

Five Layers (Bottom Up)

LayerAltitudeTemp TrendSignificance
Troposphere0–12 km↓ with heightAll weather; 75% of mass; 99% water vapour
Stratosphere12–50 km↑ with heightOzone layer (20–30 km); jet aircraft
Mesosphere50–80 km↓ with heightMeteors burn here; coldest layer (–90°C)
Thermosphere / Ionosphere80–700 km↑ with heightReflects radio waves; auroras occur here
Exosphere700–10,000 kmAtoms escapeSatellites; merges with space

Key Functions of Gases

  • O₂ — respiration, combustion
  • N₂ — dilutes oxygen, prevents spontaneous combustion
  • CO₂ — greenhouse gas; absorbs longwave radiation
  • O₃ — absorbs UV in stratosphere
  • Water vapour — source of all precipitation; latent heat carrier
  • Dust / aerosols — condensation nuclei, scattering, sunset colours

Climate Change Linkage

CO₂ rose 280 ppm (pre-industrial) → ~420 ppm (2024). Methane and N₂O also rising. Shifts the radiative balance → global warming. Anthropogenic aerosols modify cloud formation (Twomey effect).

02
Insolation & Heat Budget
PDF · Ch 21 Prelims 2010, 2012
+

Definition

Insolation = Incoming Solar Radiation reaching Earth (~1361 W/m² at top of atmosphere = solar constant). Heat Budget = balance between incoming solar (shortwave) and outgoing terrestrial (longwave) radiation that keeps Earth at ~15°C average.

Heat Budget (of 100 units incoming)

  • 35 reflected (planetary albedo): 27 by clouds + 2 atmosphere + 6 surface
  • 14 absorbed by atmosphere (ozone, water vapour, dust)
  • 51 absorbed by surface
  • Earth re-emits 65 units as longwave terrestrial radiation
  • 17 escape directly; 48 absorbed by atmosphere (greenhouse effect)
  • Net: Incoming 100 = Outgoing 100. Earth is in radiative equilibrium.

Factors Affecting Insolation

  1. Angle of sun's rays (latitude) — vertical = concentrated; slanted = diffuse
  2. Length of day — longer in summer hemisphere
  3. Atmospheric transparency — clouds, dust, water vapour absorb/scatter
  4. Distance from Sun — aphelion (July) vs perihelion (Jan)
  5. Albedo — snow reflects 80%, dark soil 5%

Climate Change Linkage

Greenhouse gases don't change incoming insolation — they trap outgoing longwave radiation, shifting the budget. Result: more retention, warmer surface. Each +1 ppm CO₂ adds measurable radiative forcing.

●Climate-Human"Discuss how deforestation and urbanisation alter regional heat budgets."

Earth is a giant solar oven; the heat budget is the bookkeeping that keeps it habitable — and climate change is the bookkeeping going wrong.

03
Albedo & Temperature Distribution
PDF · Ch 21 Prelims 2010
+

Definition

Albedo = fraction of incoming sunlight a surface reflects (0 = black/absorber; 1 = white/reflector). Earth's average albedo ~0.30.

Surface Albedo Values

SurfaceAlbedo %Implication
Fresh snow80–90Polar amplification driver
Thick cloud70–80Cooling effect
Desert / light soil30–45Hot but reflective
Grasses / vegetation20–25Moderate
Forest10–20Carbon sink + heat absorber
Oceans / water6–10Strong heat sink
Asphalt5Urban heat island driver

Descending order (ecosystems): Tundra > Taiga > Temperate Forest > Tropical Forest.

Temperature Distribution Factors

  1. Latitude — primary control
  2. Altitude — temperature falls 6.5°C/km
  3. Distance from sea — continentality
  4. Ocean currents — Gulf Stream warms NW Europe; Humboldt cools Chile
  5. Prevailing winds — onshore vs offshore
  6. Slope & aspect — sun-facing vs shaded
  7. Nature of soil/vegetation

Ice-Albedo Feedback (Climate Change)

Positive feedback loop: Warming melts ice → exposes dark ocean/land → more absorption → more warming → more melting. Why Arctic warms 4× faster than global average (polar amplification).

04
Latitudinal Heat Balance
PDF · Ch 21
+

Definition

Redistribution of heat from energy-surplus tropics (0–40°) to energy-deficit polar regions (40°–90°). Without this, equator would keep heating and poles cooling.

The Imbalance

  • 0°–40°: incoming solar > outgoing terrestrial → surplus
  • 40°–90°: outgoing terrestrial > incoming solar → deficit
  • System transports heat poleward to maintain balance

Mechanisms of Heat Transport

  1. Atmospheric circulation (60%) — Hadley, Ferrel, Polar cells; jet streams; storms
  2. Ocean circulation (40%) — Gulf Stream, Kuroshio carry heat poleward; cold currents return cold equatorward
  3. Latent heat transport — water evaporates in tropics, condenses at higher latitudes, releasing heat

Climate Change Linkage

As tropics warm faster relatively, equator-pole gradient narrows. This weakens jet streams → meandering Rossby waves → extreme winters in mid-latitudes (paradox of warming). AMOC slowdown disrupts the oceanic component.

●Locational"Why does Northwest Europe enjoy a mild climate despite its high latitude? Explain."
●Climate-Human"Disruption of latitudinal heat balance and its consequences for global agriculture."
●Climate Change"How is climate change altering the equator-to-pole temperature gradient, and what are its consequences for global weather?"
●Variability"How do meandering Rossby waves explain extreme weather episodes in mid-latitudes?"
●Current Affairs"AMOC slowdown and the future of latitudinal heat transport — examine recent research."

Lapse Rate & Atmospheric Stability
PDF · Ch 22
+

Definition

Lapse rate = rate at which temperature changes with altitude. Different types apply in different contexts.

Types of Lapse Rate

TypeRateWhat It Describes
Normal (NLR)6.5°C/kmAverage decrease in static atmosphere
Environmental (ELR)VariableActual rate measured by radiosonde
Dry Adiabatic (DALR)10°C/kmUnsaturated air parcel cooling when rising
Wet/Saturated Adiabatic (SALR)5–6°C/kmSaturated air (slower due to latent heat release)

Three Stability States

  1. Stable: ELR < SALR — rising parcel cooler than surroundings → sinks back. Calm, no clouds.
  2. Unstable: ELR > DALR — rising parcel stays warmer → continues rising. Thunderstorms.
  3. Conditionally unstable: SALR < ELR < DALR — stable for dry, unstable for saturated. Explains orographic rainfall and cloudbursts.

Why DALR > SALR?

When saturated air rises, condensation releases latent heat (540 cal/g), slowing the cooling. This released energy powers thunderstorms and cyclones.

●Climate-Human"Discuss how atmospheric instability drives the lightning crisis in eastern India (Bihar, Odisha, Jharkhand)."
●Climate Change"A warmer atmosphere increases atmospheric instability. Examine the implications for extreme convective rainfall in India."
●Variability"How does atmospheric stability vary with monsoon active and break phases?"
●Current Affairs"Recent Wayanad landslides — examine the role of orographic instability and conditional uplift."

06
Temperature Inversion
PDF · Ch 22 Mains 2013
+

Definition

Reversal of normal lapse rate — temperature increases with altitude in a specific layer (instead of decreasing). Creates a stable "lid" trapping pollutants below.

Five Types

TypeMechanismWhere/When
RadiationalSurface loses heat to clear night skyWinter nights; flat terrain
Air drainage (valley)Cold air sinks downslope into valley floorKashmir, Shimla, intermontane valleys
AdvectionalWarm air blows over cold surfaceCoastal regions; coastal fog
FrontalWarm air rides over cold air at a frontTemperate latitudes; warm fronts
SubsidenceAnticyclonic descending air compresses & warmsSubtropical highs; persistent smog

Effects

  • Smog & pollution trap — Delhi winter, Bhopal disaster, LA basin
  • Fog formation — IGP winter fog, aviation hazard
  • Suppressed rainfall — no convection, no clouds
  • Frost on valley floor — damages crops
  • Benefit: horticulture — Kashmir apples need chilling hours
  • Respiratory illness spike — PM2.5 trapped near ground

Climate Change Linkage

Warmer winters reduce frequency of strong radiational inversions. However, urbanisation increases localised inversion intensity. Delhi smog episodes increasingly severe due to inversion + crop burning + vehicular emissions.
